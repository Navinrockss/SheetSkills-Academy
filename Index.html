Excel level check – 10 quick questions

Mix of “how would you do this?” and small visuals. Answer based on how you actually work in Excel today.

1. Your manager says: “Please total this month’s sales for each branch.”
What is closest to how you would do it?
2. You have a list of customers and amounts. Boss says: “Show only top 10 customers.”
3. See this simple chart. It shows sales for Q1–Q4.
What best matches your chart skills?
4. You have a price list and an order list. You need to pull prices into the order sheet.
5. See this attendance table:
EmployeeDays PresentLate (Yes/No)
Asha26No
Rahul22Yes
Meena24No
HR asks: “Mark ‘Good Attendance’ for people with 24+ days and No in Late.” How would you do this?
6. You receive 5,000 rows of sales data. Boss says: “Show total by region and product.”
7. Your data has duplicates, extra spaces and inconsistent text (like “ north ” / “North”).
8. Manager wants a one-page view: total sales, top product, 1–2 charts.
9. Every month you get 3 similar Excel files (Jan, Feb, Mar). You must combine them.
10. You have Customers, Products and Sales as separate tables. You want one report using all three.
